## üîç Key Insights

**Crime Patterns & Timing:**
- **40,740 total crimes** with 79.7% resulting in injuries (32,472 cases)
- **2 PM, 12 PM, and 8 PM** are the most dangerous times, contributing 41.7% of all crimes
- **Weekday crimes (71%)** significantly outnumber weekend crimes (29%)
- **August shows highest crime volume** (3,507 incidents) with +286 MoM increase
- **Peak crime days**: Thursday and Friday with 800+ incidents each

**Crime Characteristics:**
- **56.6% of crimes involved weapons** (23,839 incidents)
- **86.6% resulted in property damage** (35,273 properties)
- **24.69% of cases under investigation** (14,814 cases)
- **66.90% of cases closed**, 19.60% remain open, 13.51% pending

**Geographic Distribution:**
- **California leads** with 11.2K crimes, followed by Pennsylvania (8.0K)
- **Top crime locations**: Residential areas (12.0K), Industrial areas (9.0K), University campus (7.8K)
- **Tech-related crimes are rising** in residential areas and university campuses

**Suspect & Victim Demographics:**
- **Black suspects**: 8,000 (2024) vs 6,000 (2023) - significant increase
- **Average victim age**: 52 years
- **Average suspect age**: 31 years
- **25-34 age group** shows highest suspect volume (20,971 suspects)

**Crime Types & Severity:**
- **High severity crimes**: 20.2K incidents (top priority)
- **Top crime types**: Cyber security (7.2K), Domestic offenses (7.2K), Burglary (7.2K)
- **Critical areas**: Residential areas (9.0K) and Industrial areas account for 62% of total crimes

## üí° Recommendations

**Immediate Actions:**

1. **Increase patrols during peak hours** (2 PM, 12 PM, 8 PM) which account for 41.7% of crimes
2. **Deploy additional resources on Thursdays and Fridays** (800+ crimes per day)
3. **Focus digital surveillance and cybersecurity taskforce** on rising tech-related crimes (62% of total)
4. **Prioritize burglary and drug offense crackdowns** with localized enforcement

**Strategic Initiatives:**

5. **Launch youth intervention programs** targeting 25-34 age demographic (highest suspect volume)
6. **Increase surveillance in high-crime locations**: Residential areas (12K), Industrial areas (9K), University campuses (7.8K)
7. **Focus law enforcement in California and Pennsylvania** which contribute 55.7% of all crimes
8. **Partner with local authorities** in highlighted zones for early-warning systems
9. **Expand local intervention programs** targeting youth and vulnerable populations

**Operational Improvements:**

10. **Reduce pending cases from 13.51% to <10%** through better case management
11. **Investigate August crime spike** (286 MoM increase) to identify root causes
12. **Address weekday crime concentration** (71%) with targeted weekday patrols
